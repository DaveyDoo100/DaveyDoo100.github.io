Unit 1 & 2 Vocabulary

Unit 1: Data

  • Bit: A binary digit, either 0 or 1.
  • Byte: A sequence of 8 bits.
  • Roundoff: Error when bits can’t fully represent a number.
  • Analog Data: Values that change smoothly over time.
  • Lossless: Compression that preserves all data.
  • Lossy: Compression that discards some data.
  • Metadata: Data about data, like file info.

Unit 2: Computer Systems and Networks

  • Computing Device: Any device that runs programs.
  • Computer Network: A group of connected devices.
  • Path: The route data takes between devices.
  • Protocol: Agreed-upon rules for communication.
  • Fault Tolerant: Keeps working even when parts fail.
  • Scalability: Ability to grow or shrink as needed.
  • Bandwidth: Maximum transmission capacity.
  • URL: Easy-to-remember web address.
  • IP: Assigns addresses and routes data.
  • TCP/UDP: Reliable vs. fast delivery of packets.
  • DNS: Converts domain names into IP addresses.
  • Packets & Routers: Split and forward data.
  • HTTP/WWW: Protocols for web browsing.

How the Internet Works

The internet is a massive system that connects billions of devices worldwide. Data travels in small chunks called packets, which move through routers and networks until they reach their destination. Thanks to protocols like TCP, UDP, and IP, communication is reliable, scalable, and efficient for every user.
